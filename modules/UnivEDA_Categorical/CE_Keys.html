Popularity in Middle School

  1. The goal for most kids was getting good grades, followed by being popular, and then being good in sports (Table 2; Figure 1).

    2. Table 2: Percentage of grade school children by their stated goal.

    goals
 Grades Popular  Sports 
   51.7    29.5    18.8

  2. Students feel that “being good in sports” is an important contributor to popularity as approximately 2/3rds of studens ranked this as either “most important” or “somewhat important” to being popular (Table 3; Figure 2).

    3. Table 3: Percentage of grade school children by their rank of the impiortance of being good in sports (1=most important).

d <- read.csv("PopularKids.csv")
tbl1 <- xtabs(~goals,data=d)
( ptbl1 <- percTable(tbl1,digits=1,addMargins=FALSE) )
barplot(ptbl1,xlab="Student's Goal",ylab="Percentage")
tbl2 <- xtabs(~sports,data=d)
( ptbl2 <- percTable(tbl2,digits=1,addMargins=FALSE) )
barplot(ptbl2,xlab="Rank of Sports in Popularity",ylab="Percentage")
tbl3 <- xtabs(~type,data=d)
( ptbl3 <- percTable(tbl3,digits=1,addMargins=FALSE) )
