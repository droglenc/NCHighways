Increased Civility

  1. \(\alpha\)=0.05.
  2. \(H_{A}: \mu<4104\) and \(H_{0}: \mu=4104\), where \(\mu\) is the mean number of citizens killed by ALL nation-states.
  3. 1-sample t-test because (i) one population (all nation-states) was sampled, (ii) a quantitative variable (number of killings) was recorded, and (iii) \(\sigma\) is not known.
  4. An observational study with clear randomization of 75 countries.
  5. The assumptions are met because (i) \(\sigma\) is not known and (ii) n=75>40.
  6. \(\bar{x}\)=3292.8 killings.
  7. t=-1.4592 with 74 df.
  8. p-value=\(0.0744\).
  9. Do not reject \(H_{0}\) because the p-value>\(\alpha\).
  10. It appears that the number of killings for recent times is NOT significanlty lower than it was as determined from 19th century history books.
  11. Strinctly not needed, but one could say that they were 95% confident that the number of killings in recent years for ALL nation-states is less than 4219 (which contains the value in the \(H_{0}\)).

Thickness of the Ozone Layer

  1. \(\alpha=0.01\)
  2. \(H_{O}:\mu=300\), \(H_{A}:\mu\neq300\), where \(\mu\) is the mean thickness of the ozone layer from ALL locations.
  3. A one-sample t-test is required because (i) a quantitative variable (thickness) was measured, (ii)the individuals are from one population (the ozone layer), and (iii) \(\sigma\) is not known. Thus, the population mean is being compared to a specific value in the null hypothesis.
  4. An observational study of 45 random samples of the ozone was conducted.
  5. The assumptions are met because \(n=45>40\) and \(\sigma\) is UNknown.
  6. \(\bar{x}=\) 345 (Table 1).

  8. The p-value \(<0.00005\) (Table 1).
  9. The \(H_{O}\) is rejected because the \(p-value<\alpha\) (Table 1).
  10. The mean thickness of the ozone layer at ALL locations appears to be different (specifically greater) than 300 Dobson units.
  11. I am 99% confident that the mean thickness of the ozone layer in ALL locations between 337 and 354 Dobson Units (Table 1).
