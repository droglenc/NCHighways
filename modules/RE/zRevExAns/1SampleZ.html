Cactus Growth

  1. α = 0.05.
  2. H0: μ = 4, HA: μ < 4 where μ is the mean growth of all cacti.
  3. A one-sample Z-test is required because (i) a quantitative variable (growth) was measured, (ii) individuals from only one group/population were sampled (only one set of conditions considered), (iii) the population mean is compared to a specific value in the null hypothesis, and (iv) σ is known (given in the background).
  4. The data are part of an experiment in which cacti were randomly selected.
  5. σ is known. In addition, because the population distribution is normal (stated in the background), we do not have to worry about the size of the sample.
  6. x¯ = 3.26.
  7. Z = \(\frac{3.26-4}{\frac{1.40}{\sqrt{10}}}\) = \(\frac{-0.74}{0.44}\) = -1.67.
  8. The p-value is \(0.0473\).
  9. H0 is rejected because the p-value<α.
  10. The mean growth rate of all cacti under the experimental conditions does appear to be less than 4 cm.
  11. A 95% upper confidence bound is warranted in this situation and will use a Z∗ = 1.645. Thus, 3.26+1.645*0.44 or 3.26+0.72 = 3.98. Thus, one is 95% confident that the mean growth of all cacti under the experimental conditions is less than 3.98 cm.

R Appendix

( pval <- distrib(3.26,mean=4,sd=1.40/sqrt(10)) )
( zstar <- distrib(0.95,type="q") )

Length of Northern Pike

  1. α = 0.05. (Note that this is obtained from working backwards from the 95% confidence statement.)
  2. H0: μ = 600, HA: μ &neq; 600 where μ is the mean length of all pike in Chivyrkui Bay, Lake Baikal.
  3. A one-sample Z-test is required because a (i) quantitative variable (length) was measured, (ii) individuals from only one group/population were sampled (Chivyrkui Bay, Lake Baikal), (iii) the population mean is compared to a specific value in the null hypothesis, and (iv) σ is known (given in the background).
  4. Observational study with n = 30 pike. No obvious randomization.
  5. n = 30 ≥ 15 and the population is not strongly skewed (background says “slightly”" right-skewed); σ is known.
  6. x¯ = 656.1.
  7. Z = \(\frac{656.1-600}{\frac{130}{\sqrt{30}}}\) = 2.363.
  8. The p-value is \(0.0181\).
  9. Reject H0 because the p-value<α.
  10. The true mean length of all pike in Lake Baikal appears to be different than 600 mm.
  11. A 95% confidence interval is warranted in this situation and will use a Z∗ = ± 1.960. Thus, 656.1 ± 1.96*23.74, 656.1 ± 46.53, or (609.57, 702.63). Therefore, one is 95% confident that true mean length of all pike in Lake Baikal is between 609.6 and 702.6 mm.

R Appendix

( pval <- 2*distrib(656.1,mean=600,sd=130/sqrt(30),lower.tail=FALSE) )
( zstar <- distrib(0.05/2,type="q",lower.tail=FALSE) )

Chronic Myofascial Pain Syndrome

  1. α = 0.10.
  2. H0: μ = 2.5, HA: μ > 2.5 where μ is the mean length of pain.
  3. A one-sample Z-test is required because (i) a quantitative variable (length of pain) was measured, (ii) individuals from only one group/population were sampled (patients with this syndrome), (iii) the population mean is compared to a specific value in the null hypothesis, and (iv) σ is known (given in the background).
  4. An observational study that is not obviously random was used.
  5. The population is normally distributed (given in the background) and σ is known (= 0.5). Thus, the test statistic below should follow a normal distribution.
  6. x¯ = 2.77 years (Table 1).
  7. Z = 1.31 (Table 1).
  8. p-value = \(0.0957\) (Table 1).
  9. Reject H0 because the p-value<α.
  10. It appears that the mean duration of pain for all patients may be longer than 2.5 years.
  11. A 90% lower confidence bound is used here. Thus, one is 90% confident that the mean duration of pain for all patients is more than 2.51 years (Table 1).

Cholesterol Levels

  1. α = 0.10.
  2. H0: μ = 190, HA: μ &neq; 190 where μ is the mean cholesterol level of Asian women.
  3. A one-sample Z-test is required because (i) a quantitative variable (cholesterol level) was measured, (ii) individuals from only one group/population were sampled (Asian women aged 21-40 that had recently immigrated to the U.S.), (iii) the population mean is compared to a specific value in the null hypothesis, and (iv) σ is known (given in the background).
  4. An observational study with randomly selected women was used.
  5. n = 40 ≥ 30 and σ is known.
  6. x¯ = 180.55 mg/dl (Table 2).
  7. Z = -1.49 (Table 2).
  8. p-value = \(0.1352\) (Table 2).
  9. The H0 is not rejected because the p-value>α.
  10. The true mean cholesterol level for all Asian women aged 21-40 that recently immigrated to the U.S. does not appear to be different than 190 mg/dl.
