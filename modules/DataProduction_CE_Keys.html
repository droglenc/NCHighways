Study Types I

  1. Experiment … the people were given a treatment (placebo or fish oil)
  2. Experiment … the men were given a treatment (training program) with before/after results recorded
  3. Observational … the women were “followed” over time, no treatment was imparted (the B12 deficiency or not was naturally induced)
  4. Observational … no treatment was imparted (the brain cancer factor was naturally induced)
  5. Experimental … a treatment (animations or not) was imparted
  6. Observational … no treatment was imparted (alcohol use was a choice of the women)

Types of Observational Studies

  1. Convenience sample.
  2. Voluntary response.
  3. Simple random sample (probability-based sample).
  4. Voluntary response.
  5. Convenience sample.

Softball Bats and Balls

  1. Experimental … imparting treatments (ball types and bats were chosen specifically)
  2. Individual .. a “hit”
  3. Below
    1. Response … Distance the ball was hit.
    2. Factors … Brand of ball and type of bat
    3. Levels … 2 brands of ball and 3 types of bat
    4. Treatments … 6
    5. Replicates … 4 (balls of each brand by each type of bat)
  4. I would first randomly order the bats and then randomly order the two brands for each bat.

Identify Response and Explanatory Variables

  1. Response is “success rate”, explanatory is distance of putt.
  2. Response is “response time”, explanatory is amount of time awake.
  3. Response is growth, explanatory is amount of fertilizer.
  4. It is not obvious which is the response or expanatory … would depend on the researcher’s question.
  5. Response is sales of umbrellas, explanatory is amount of rainfall.
  6. Response is weight, explanatory is age.
