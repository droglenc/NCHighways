Background

Measurements of the levels of arsenic in the drinking water, cooking water, and toenail samples, as well as related covariates, were measured on 21 individuals with private wells in a New Hampshire community. The variables below were recorded in the Arsenic.csv file located on the R Resources web page.

  • age: Age (yrs) of person
  • sex: Sex of person
  • usedrink: Household well used for drinking – A=“\(<\frac{1}{4}\)”, B=“\(\approx\frac{1}{4}\)”, C=“\(\approx\frac{1}{2}\)”, D=“\(\approx\frac{3}{4}\)”, E=“\(>\frac{3}{4}\)”
  • usecook: Household well used for cooking – A=“\(<\frac{1}{4}\)”, B=“\(\approx\frac{1}{4}\)”, C=“\(\approx\frac{1}{2}\)”, D=“\(\approx\frac{3}{4}\)”, E=“\(>\frac{3}{4}\)”
  • arswater: Arsenic in water (ppm)
  • arsnails: Arsenic in toenails (ppm)
