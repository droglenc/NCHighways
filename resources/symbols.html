The following are symbols that you will encounter in this course. They are ordered by roughly the order in which you will encounter the symbol.

n
  • Sample size.
N
  • Population size.
\(\bar{x}\)
  • Sample mean (average) of the X variable.
μ
  • Population mean.
s
  • Sample standard deviation.
σ
  • Population standard deviation.
s2
  • Sample variance.
Q1
  • First quartile.
Q3
  • Third quartile.
IQR
  • Interquartile range (Q3-Q1).
X~N(μ,σ)
  • The variable X is normally distributioned with a mean of μ and standard deviation of σ.
N(0,1)
  • Standard normal distribution.
Z
    1. A variable that follows a N(0,1). (ii) The test statistic in a one-sample Z-test.
r
    1. The sample correlation coefficient. (ii) The number of rows in a two-way frequency table.
c
  • The number of columns in a two-way frequency table.
α
    1. The rejection criterion. (ii) The probability of making a Type I error.
β
  • The probability of making a Type II error.
M
  • The sample median.
