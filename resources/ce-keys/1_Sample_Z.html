Cancer Tumors

  1. \(\alpha\)=0.10.
  2. \(H_{A}: \mu=27\) and \(H_{0}: \mu=27\), where \(\mu\) is the mean time of recurrence for ALL bladder cancer patients that received the placebo.
  3. 1-sample Z-test because (i) one population (bladder cancer patients that received the placebo) was sampled, (ii) a quantitative variable (time of recurrence) was recorded, and (iii) \(\sigma\) is assumed to be known (=15).
  4. Loosely an “experiment” as the subjects were given a placebo treatment. There is no obvious randomization apparent in this study.
  5. The assumptions are met because (i) \(\sigma\) is known (=15) and (ii) n=42>30.
  6. \(\bar{x}\)=31.7 months.
  7. t=2.0306.
  8. p-value=\(0.0211\).
  9. Reject \(H_{0}\) because the p-value<\(\alpha\).
  10. It appears that the time of recurrence for the tumor for ALL patients that received the placebo is greater than the hypothesized 27 months.
  11. I am 90% confident that the time of recurrence for the tumor for ALL patients that received the placebo is greater than 28.73 months.

Raptors at Hawk’s Ridge

  1. \(\alpha=0.01\)
  2. \(H_{O}:\mu=50000\), \(H_{A}:\mu>50000\), where \(\mu\) is the mean number of raptors seen in ALL years at Hawk’s Ridge.
  3. A one-sample Z-test is required because (i) a quantitative variable (number of raptors) was measured, (ii)the individuals are from one population (all years at Hawk’s Ridge), and (iii) \(\sigma\) is known (=37000). Thus, the population mean is being compared to a specific value in the null hypothesis.
  4. An observational sample of years that was not random was taken.
  5. The assumptions are met because \(n=32>30\) and \(\sigma\) is known.
  6. \(\bar{x}=\) 68183 (Table 1).
