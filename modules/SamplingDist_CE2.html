Expectations Based on the CLT

The Central Limit Theorem (CLT) provides a mechanism for understanding what the sampling distribution of the sample means will look like. The specifics of the CLT are in the module reading (and should be in your module preparation notes).

In this exercise, you will be asked to identify what the CLT says the sampling distribution of sample means should look like for different shapes of the population distribution and sample sizes (n). Specifically, you will describe the shape (normal or not), center (with a specific value), and dispersion (with a specific value) for each situation.
