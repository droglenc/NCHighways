Forest Composition

  1. There were 15 stands that last had a fire between 200 and 300 years ago (Table 1).
  2. The percentage of deciduous tree stands with a fire in the last 100 years is 37.5% (Table 3).
  3. The percentage of mixed-coniferous tree stands with a fire between 200 and 300 years ago is 50.0% (Table 3).
  4. The percentage of all stands that were coniferous with a fire between 200 and 300 years ago is 14.6% (Table 4).
  5. The percentage of tree stands that had a fire between 200 and 300 years ago that were coniferous is 46.7% (Table 2).
  6. The percentage of all stands that were deciduous with a fire between 100 and 200 years ago is 31.2% (Table 4).
  7. The percentage of mixed-deciduous stands that had a fire within the last 100 years is 15.4% (Table 3).
  8. Those with more recent fires tended to be deciduous, those without recent fires tended to be coniferous.

Class Excercise 3

The questions below use the following tables.

  1. There were 119 students from the fourth grade in the sample.
  2. The percentage of All students in the fifth grade with the goal “to be popular’ was 11.5% (Table 8).
  3. The percentage of fourth grade students with the goal “to be good in sports” was 21.0% (Table 6).
  4. The percentage of fifth grade students with the goal “to make good grades” was 50.0% (Table 6).
  5. The percentage of all students with the goal to “make good grades” was 51.7% (Table 8).
  6. The percentage of students with the goal “to be popular” that were in the sixth grade was 39.0% (Table 7).
  7. Fairly even distribution of students into the goals categories among the grades. Thus, there is no real difference in goals by grade in school.
