Coin Purse

  1. The probability of randomly selecting a nickel from this purse is 0.531 (i.e., \(\frac{17}{32}\)).
  2. The probability of randomly selecting a dime from this purse is 0.469 (i.e., \(\frac{15}{32}\)).
  3. The probability of randomly selecting a dime if two nickels and three dimes had been removed is 0.444 (i.e., \(\frac{12}{27}\)).

Greenhouse Plants

  1. The probability of randomly selected a tomato plant is 0.333 (i.e., \(\frac{10}{30}\)).
  2. The probability of randomly selected a cauliflower plant is 0.267 (i.e., \(\frac{8}{30}\)).
  3. The probability of randomly selected a pea plant assuming that all tomato plants were gone is 0.600 (i.e., \(\frac{12}{20}\)).

Lengths of Pine Needles

  1. The probability that a randomly selected needle is between 70 and 80 mm long is 0.468.
  2. The probability that a randomly selected needle is longer than 90 mm is 0.030.
  3. The probability that a randomly selected needle is less than 50 mm is 0.0009.
